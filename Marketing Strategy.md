If you structure your plans like this:

- **Basic** → 5 users, 30 days  
- **Pro** → 15 users, 90 days  
- **Enterprise** → unlimited users, 365 days  

then the marketing angle should highlight **progressive value** and **clear upgrade incentives**. Here’s how I’d recommend positioning them:

---

### 🔹 Marketing Strategy

1. **Basic Plan (Entry Point)**  
   - Emphasize affordability and simplicity.  
   - Messaging: *“Perfect for small teams starting out — 5 users covered, one month to try everything risk‑free.”*  
   - Goal: Get clients in the door quickly, low barrier to entry.

2. **Pro Plan (Growth Stage)**  
   - Position as the “sweet spot” for growing businesses.  
   - Messaging: *“Scale your team with 15 users and 3 months of coverage — more time, more flexibility, more insights.”*  
   - Goal: Encourage upgrades by showing how Pro solves the pain of outgrowing Basic.

3. **Enterprise Plan (Premium Tier)**  
   - Market as “worry‑free, unlimited, long‑term.”  
   - Messaging: *“Unlimited users, full year coverage, and premium support — designed for organizations that want stability and confidence.”*  
   - Goal: Appeal to larger companies and decision‑makers who value reliability and prestige.

---

### 🔹 Psychological Hooks
- **Scarcity & urgency**: Basic expires in 30 days → creates urgency to renew or upgrade.  
- **Social proof**: Show case studies of teams that grew from Basic → Pro → Enterprise.  
- **Prestige**: Enterprise should feel like a VIP club (dedicated manager, SLA guarantee).  
- **Upsell triggers**:  
  - When a Basic license hits 5 users, prompt: *“Upgrade to Pro to unlock 10 more seats and longer coverage.”*  
  - When Pro hits 90 days, prompt: *“Enterprise ensures you never worry about renewals again.”*

---

### 🔹 Visual Marketing Ideas
- Comparison chart showing **users vs. duration** side by side.  
- Highlight “Recommended” badge on Pro (most attractive balance).  
- Use color coding: teal for Basic, gold for Pro, platinum for Enterprise.  





---

### ✅ Recommendation
- Make **Pro** the most attractive option (longer duration + more users at a reasonable price).  
- Use **Basic** as a low‑risk entry point.  
- Position **Enterprise** as prestige, unlimited, and long‑term stability.
